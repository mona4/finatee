        • Every Friday we will be raising a new issue the world is facing. From water conservative to foeticide,deforestation to child marriage, every Friday this DRISTI page will be dedicated to these issues ,which can't be neglected. So we thought we should start with jharkhand and it's capital RANCHI itself. " Water water everywhere " --------------------------------------- Water,the basic necessity for our sustenance is on the verge on depletion and you all are well aware of it. According to a TOI survey, 22 out of 32 big cities in India face water crisis. The condition in Jharkhand is no less pathetic and leads to a even more deplorable state. Jharkhand ‘s capital Ranchi, a city of 11 lakh people has declared a water emergency. In December last year, the state government declared drought in the state. It is among the 11 states in the country reeling under drought. The city needs 45 million gallons of water daily. But one out of the three dams that supply water to the city is almost dry and the water levels in the other two are below the mean for this time of the year. This means that just about half of Ranchi’s total water requirement is met every day. Problem 1: A government residential school in Ranchi’s Bundu block is almost empty even in the middle of an academic session Just 60 odd students out of the total 250 remain. The rest left for home a month ago. The reason was the school’s hand pumps which refused to work despite several maintenances. Water was finally found at 100 feet instead of the usual 70 after borewell but with a drought in Jharkhand even this may not be enough for the residents as earlier a borewell had gone dry too. For students of the school who stayed behind, yellow and dirty water being supplied in the town by government tankers was the only option for over a month. Problem 2 :- About 40 kilometres away, in Ranchi town, a similar situation plagues the Urdu Hindi Primary School. Students here are spending their days at the neighbourhood handpump, filling water for the school’s midday meals and the toilets.School officials say there has been no repair despite repeated reminders to the education department.The officials, however, in the education department have promised they would look into the matter and make alternative arrangements soon. But there has been no comment on why they had not acted on the complaint so far .Meanwhile, there is not even a drop of water in the school. Problem 3:- For over two months now Ranchi Resident Rita Devi has missed out on work every third day. She is a municipal officer and lives with her husband and two children in Ranchi ‘s Hindpidhi, a largely middle class locality. Once in every three days, Ms Devi and many other people in her locality queue up early in the morning to wait, sometimes till noon, for the government tanker that brings water for the locality. “But at the end of the day, I call myself lucky if I can fill even two buckets of water. Sometimes, we have to call in expensive private water tankers. There is no other way,” she says. With the scanty Rainfall and water level depleting inches by inches, let's STEP FORWARD AND BRING ABOUT a change and Conserve water! Conserve the remaining minuscule existence of life on this planet!

BIT Placement & Interview Experiences
11 mins ·

#173
‪#‎BITCOMPANY‬: Oracle Financial Services (On campus placement)

Name: Shrivats Singh
Batch: 2k12...
Branch: EEE
Campus: Mesra
Profile: Associate Consultant
Email Id: shrivatssingh@gmail.com

ROUND 1: (ONLINE APTITUDE TEST)
PLATFORM: Oracle Based Platform

It was an online test with questions covering sections on quantitative aptitude, verbal ability, reasoning and there was a section on coding aptitude & algorithm development. All the questions were MCQs type & Oracle's personalized test platform was hassle free. A thorough practice of aptitude questions from standard text-books is recommended as it is an eliminator round. Regarding coding aptitude, no syntax related stuff is asked & no requirement of coding skills on platforms like C++/java is required. Logical reasoning & cool approach will help you clear this round.

ROUND 2: Technical Interview 1

We had to fill a form & carry it along with our resumes. The interviewer was very friendly. The first question was why you want to jump from a core branch to a consulting domain. This is a very important question as it seeks to know your careers' objectives and one needs to be prepared beforehand. Make sure you happen to be there by choice and not by chance and your answer to this question must prove this. It was followed with a walk through your resume round. Again, this is a common question of interviews. Make sure you know what you have written in your resume thoroughly. Even an achievement can lead you to trouble if you cannot explain every nook and corner of it. Then questions checking basic economics/finance aptitude were put forward. Questions like tell me all the interpretations you can make if you were given day to day data of Gold prices in India for the last five years. However the questions of this round may vary from person to person depending upon the contents of resume.

ROUND 3: Technical Interview 2

It was a thorough round lasting for around 20 minutes. There was a set of 3 quantitative aptitude questions. (LOD 3 level of Arun Sharma) I was asked to solve them by writing the steps & explaining them. The emphasis is on your thought process & even though you cant reach an answer, a logical approach would be sufficient to impress the interviewer. However I could answer all of them, so I think it covered up my other mistakes in interview. It was followed with an innovative-acumen checking question which I found to be the most interesting. The question was, implement your own solution to any of the local demands( it may be a solution to campus needs of residents of your college) & explain the parameters you would take in developing the program. This was an amazing question checking logic & he let me answer it for 5 minutes. It was followed with a simple algorithm on swapping of two variables. The interview concluded with questions from the project I had done & I found that the interviewer was laying emphasis on the uniqueness/innovative quotient of work.
This round is the decider round & a sharp presence of mind would help you nailing it.

ROUND 4: HR Interview

It covered basic HR questions, tell me about yourself, why Oracle, why shifting to Consulting domain, why not core job, etc. Make sure that you know the job profile & about the company thoroughly. Also don't criticize other jobs, just describe how the job profile aligns with your career objectives. (Doing that of-course would require that you indeed have those career objectives)
It is not very tough round to crack and cool mind-set would help you sail through your Oracle dreams. Good Luck!
